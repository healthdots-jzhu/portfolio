# Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** January 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how this Portfolio Platform collects, uses, and protects your information when you use this Website.

---

## 1. Information We Collect

We collect the following types of information:

* **Authentication Data:** Your email address and authentication credentials are handled by Amazon Cognito. We store your user account identifier (subject/sub claim) and the identity provider (issuer) used for authentication (Cognito, Google, Facebook, etc.).
* **Account Information:** Your email address, last login timestamp, and user profile metadata
* **Portfolio Content:** All content you create and upload, including text, images, code samples, project descriptions, and associated metadata
* **Usage Data:** Access logs, portfolio views, and interaction patterns with the Website
* **Technical Data:** IP address, browser type, device information, and pages visited
* **Cookies:** Small files stored on your device to maintain session state and improve functionality

---

## 2. How We Use Information

We use collected information to:

* Authenticate you and manage your account across multiple identity providers
* Store and display your portfolio content via AWS cloud infrastructure
* Personalize your experience and provide portfolio management features
* Monitor and analyze Website performance and usage trends
* Prevent fraud and enforce our Terms of Use
* Respond to your inquiries or support requests
* Comply with legal obligations and law enforcement requests
* Analyze aggregated and anonymized data for statistics, market trends, and insights without exposing individual identities
* Use anonymized data for sales, marketing, and promotional purposes

---

## 3. Identity Providers and Authentication

We use Amazon Cognito to manage user authentication. Cognito supports authentication through:

* Native Cognito user pools
* Federated identity providers (Google, Facebook, and other OIDC/SAML providers)

When you authenticate, we store:
* Your subject identifier (sub) from the identity provider
* The issuer URL (e.g., Cognito authority or Google's OAuth endpoint)
* Your email address
* The provider hint (cognito, google, facebook, etc.)
* Your last login timestamp

This allows you to use multiple authentication methods while maintaining a single account.

By using this Website, you acknowledge that your data is processed and stored on AWS infrastructure and subject to AWS's security practices, data center policies, and data protection standards.
